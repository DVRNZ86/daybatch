# IDEAS.md — parking lot

New ideas land here with a one-line rationale. Nothing exits mid-phase; entries are considered between phases only.

## Pre-seeded (Stage D candidates, premium hard modes)

- **Endless Crossing** — continuous boards until lives exhausted; score = longest run; personal best tracked. Rationale: natural "one more go" premium mode built from existing Crossing logic.
- **Codebreak: Repeats** — symbols may repeat (16,807 codes), 10 guesses; duplicate-letter Wordle verdict rules. Rationale: proven hard-mode formula, big difficulty jump with minimal new UI.

- **UI modernisation pass (B5 candidate)** — depth via layered shadows, springy tap micro-interactions, actually load Space Grotesk/IBM Plex Mono (CSS names them but never loads them), dark mode, richer tab chips. Rationale: current look is v13-faithful by contract; a deliberate polish pass fits B5's scope. (Filed 11 Jul 2026 from Darren's feedback.)
- **Crossing difficulty tuning** — on ~13% of seeds (measured over 2,000) the first tap's zero-cascade reveals a safe corridor to the flag, making the round a deduction-free walk; on other days it plays as intended. Levers: cap cascade reach, bias trap layout so the goal row stays hidden, tune the 34% trap density. Changes generation → breaks same-date-same-puzzle, so it needs its own approved phase and a fresh seed contract. (Filed 11 Jul 2026 from Darren's playtest.)
- **Label the two streaks (B5 candidate)** — Darren's first reaction to 🔥2 (header, batch streak: ≥1 game/day) vs "✨ Perfect batch — streak 1" (all five/day) was "what's the difference?" Real users will ask the same. Options: tooltip/tap-hint on the 🔥 chip, or rename the report line to "All-five streak". (Filed 12 Jul 2026 from Darren's phone test.)
- **Crossing Retry erases the daily attempt** — Retry mid-game wipes progress with no trace, and after a finish it clears the result bar so the first attempt can't be re-shared from the board (the history record itself survives — first completion stands — but no UI surfaces it until B5's stats screen). Options: remove Retry for dailies (keep for practice), or lock the first attempt's result as the shareable one and treat retries as unscored replays. Needs a decision on daily-replay semantics. (Filed 11 Jul 2026 from Darren's playtest.)
- **Lexi content depth** — only 222 of the 430 six-letter dictionary words qualify as puzzles (7–16 targets), and the daily hash can repeat one at any time. Two levers: expand the 6-letter dictionary (every qualifying addition = a new puzzle) and/or add no-repeat daily scheduling once history storage exists (B2+). Rationale: ~7 months of unique dailies is thin if the game gets traction. (Filed 11 Jul 2026.)
- **Lexi dictionary gaps + bonus words** — the curated 2,425-word list is US-spelled and has holes (Darren's playtest: "teen" and "centre" rejected; "center" present). Bigger design gap: only the day's *target* words are accepted — any other legitimate word shakes red with no credit. Consider a broader validation dictionary with a "bonus words" counter (Wordscapes convention), separate from the curated target list. Changes accepted-word behaviour → needs a phase + approval. (Filed 11 Jul 2026 from Darren's playtest.)
- **Accessibility pass (B5 candidate)** — B4's Lighthouse run (accessibility category, out of scope for the "PWA green" acceptance bar) surfaced pre-existing gaps inherited from v13's structure: game-grid buttons have no accessible name, several text/background pairs fail contrast, viewport meta sets `user-scalable=no`, and there's no `<main>` landmark. Rationale: fits naturally alongside the already-planned UI modernisation pass rather than a one-off fix mid-B4. (Filed 12 Jul 2026 from the B4 Lighthouse run.)

## Stage D — monetization & go-to-market (spec'd, see PLAN.md A9/D1)

- **Monetization model + marketing plan** — Darren's original brief (before this repo existed) asked for a plan to "implement, market and monetise." Now specified: three-tier pricing ($2/mo, $20/yr, $30 lifetime), redemption-code entitlement, no accounts — see PLAN.md A9 (architecture) and D1 (phase/acceptance criteria). Still outstanding: the actual go-to-market/marketing timeline itself, anchored on the 10 Sep 2026 launch (EPOCH). (Filed 12 Jul 2026, updated 12 Jul 2026 once D1 was spec'd.)
- **Web-only vs. native wrap (Capacitor)** — decided for now: stay web-only (PWA + Stripe, no accounts) deliberately, to build traction before spending on a native wrap. Not permanent — a future Capacitor wrap would unlock native App/Play Store IAP + store discovery, but it's a real architecture shift, out of scope until web traction actually justifies it. Revisit once there's a real signal (retained users, revenue from D1) worth the engineering cost. (Filed 12 Jul 2026, decided 12 Jul 2026, see PLAN.md A9.)
- **Sonar real-time multiplayer ("play another user")** — parked explicitly, contingent on demonstrated traction after D1 ships. Real head-to-head play needs a realtime backend (matchmaking, live state sync) — a fundamentally larger and different build than anything else in D1 (which is entirely client-side + one stateless verify function). Do not build until there's real demand. (Filed 12 Jul 2026 from Darren's D1 scoping.)
- **Streak/history transfer on device change** — the premium code transfers entitlement, but streaks/history are device-local by design (no accounts): a customer's new phone starts at streak zero. Two mitigation levers, in ascending effort: (1) local export/import of the storage root — zero backend, natural home beside the code in the Manage view; (2) code-keyed cloud snapshot in Worker KV — possession of the code proves ownership, but this puts personal play data on the server for the first time and needs a two-device merge story. Original Wordle lived with local-only streaks for years, so (1) is likely enough. (Filed 19 Jul 2026 from Darren's D1 device-transfer question.)
